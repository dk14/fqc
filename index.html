⚡️ Why we are skeptical about quantum computing

The author of this project demonstrates that the hype surrounding quantum computers vastly exceeds their practical, near‑term capabilities. A detailed critique through actual determinstic (and no-information exchange) simulation of quantum entanglement, is at q.doomsdayexplorer.online . It shows that quantum effects can be modeled fully and deterministically - no magic. It uses pulse-width modulation - the key trick quantum theory makes you believe in is that there is no communication happening, while in reality coincednce detector (modeled in q, as wellas its equivalents in more complex experiments) is hiddenly designed to be communication point in everty quantum experiment.

Moreover: the qubits we currently can build are noisy, expensive and nowhere near the scale required for the algorithms that are often advertised. Relying on “quantum supremacy” for real‑world quantitative research is, at best, a gamble. Quantum computer without noise (or noise minimized), still would be worse than analog computer (without noise) in its performance.

⚡️ Why the reproduction of CHSH‑type experiment on q.doomsdayexplorer.online is **the opposite of** proof of quantum entanglement

The demo runs a fully deterministic, classical simulation of a CHSH Bell‑test. The apparent violation of the CHSH inequality is produced entirely by the **coincidence‑detector’s post‑selection rule**, not by any non‑local quantum correlation. No hidden communication occurs between the two wings of the experiment; the skewed statistics are an artifact of the detector’s comparison procedure.

Consequently, the “magic” that many presentations claim as evidence of quantum entanglement can be reproduced (and therefore refuted) with a trivial classic model. The result weakens the argument that such experiments demonstrate an inherent quantum superiority.

Finding equivalences of coincidence detector trick (both humans and AI agents might be capable of) in other experiments can take care of the rest, and refute quantum mechanics completely, leaving only what actually works - Digital Signal Processing.
